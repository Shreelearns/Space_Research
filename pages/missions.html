Our Missions

Explore the groundbreaking missions that are expanding human knowledge and capability beyond Earth's atmosphere. From lunar exploration to deep space observation, SpaceLink Research is pioneering the future.

Artemis Gateway

Ongoing

Started: 2024 | Estimated Completion: 2028

Establishing a sustainable human presence on the Moon as a stepping stone for Mars exploration.

Key Objectives

  • Deploy lunar gateway station
  • Establish sustained habitation
  • Resource prospecting and mining
  • Test deep space operations
Budget: $8.2B

Europa Clipper

Upcoming

Launch: 2025 | Mission Duration: 4 years

Investigating Jupiter's moon Europa for signs of habitability in its subsurface ocean.

Key Objectives

  • Study subsurface ocean composition
  • Analyze potential for life
  • Map ice shell characteristics
  • Search for water plumes
Budget: $5.2B

James Webb Observations

Completed

Duration: 2022 - 2024

Revolutionary infrared observations of the earliest galaxies in the universe.

Key Achievements

  • Captured first galaxies at z>20
  • Discovered 47 new exoplanets
  • Published 250+ research papers
  • Revolutionized cosmological models
Budget: $10B

Mars Rover Alpha

Ongoing

Deployed: 2022 | Planned End: 2026

Advanced rover exploring Valles Marineris canyon system for geological insights and signs of ancient water.

Key Objectives

  • Drill and analyze subsurface samples
  • Study geological history
  • Search for biosignatures
  • Test habitat equipment
Budget: $3.8B

SkyWatch Constellation

Upcoming

Deployment: 2025-2026 | 144 satellites total

Next-generation satellite constellation for global climate monitoring and disaster response coordination.

Key Objectives

  • Real-time climate data collection
  • Disaster monitoring and alerts
  • Ocean temperature tracking
  • Atmospheric composition analysis
Budget: $2.1B

Cosmic Lens Observatory

Completed

Duration: 2020 - 2024

Space-based gravitational lens telescope for studying distant galaxies and dark matter distribution.

Key Achievements

  • Mapped dark matter filaments
  • Discovered 12 new galaxy clusters
  • Advanced lensing theory by 40%
  • 100+ international collaborations
Budget: $4.5B
